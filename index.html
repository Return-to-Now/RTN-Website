How it works

A few small interventions that actually change behavior.

Scroll to see how
Step 01

The widget intercepts the reflex.

A home screen widget replaces social app icons. The moment someone on your team reaches for Instagram or TikTok, they see Return to Now first. That single pause is enough to break the automatic grab-and-scroll cycle before the app ever opens.

One moment of friction is all it takes to interrupt an automatic behavior and return choice to the user.
No content is blocked. The app is still one tap away. The goal is awareness, not restriction.
Step 02

They set an intention before opening.

If they choose to open the app, they say why. A quick prompt asks what they're looking for — and reminds them of the things they said matter most to them. Then they set a time limit and proceed.

Intentions are personal. Each person decides for themselves what they want more of. Nothing is prescribed.
Time limits are self-set, not enforced. The app trusts the person to follow through.
Step 03

A timer keeps them honest with themselves.

While using the app, a quiet background timer runs. When time is up, Return to Now asks if they're done — or if they want to extend. It's not a hard wall. It's a gentle, persistent reminder of the commitment they made to themselves.

No hard cutoffs. They stay in control at every moment. The app nudges, not nags.
Session data stays private. Usage details are never visible to the employer.
Step 04

A quick check-in builds self-awareness over time.

After a session, a brief reflection prompt asks how it went. Did they stick to their intention? Over time, this builds genuine insight into their own patterns — and their own motivation to change. No score, no shame.

Progress is personal. Streaks and patterns are shown only to the user.
Gradual habit change compounds. Small daily wins add up to a meaningfully different relationship with their phone.

The philosophy

Built on behavioral science, not willpower.

Every feature in Return to Now is grounded in research on how people actually change their habits — not how we wish they would.

01
Pattern interruption

The widget creates a deliberate pause between the impulse and the action. That single moment of friction is enough to disrupt automatic behavior and return control to them.

02
Habit replacement

Your people identify what they want more time for before they restrict anything. The alternative is ready before the craving hits — replacing a behavior, not just removing one.

03
Implementation intentions

Stating "I will do X when Y happens" dramatically improves follow-through. Every intention set in Return to Now is a small, specific plan — which is exactly what makes them stick.
